Enrolment pathways

Three ways to participate

Unlike most advanced trainings, many of our 300hr modules are open to everyone — not just certified teachers. Here's how students typically engage with the programme.

Deepen your practice

You're a dedicated practitioner who wants to explore yoga anatomy, philosophy, or sequencing at an advanced level. No teaching certification required for most modules. Perfect for curious minds who simply want to go deeper.

Earn CE credits

You hold a 200hr (or higher) certification and want to maintain or expand your Yoga Alliance registration with Continuing Education (CE) hours. Each module delivers between 15 and 35 CE hours.

Full 300hr certification

You hold a 200hr CYT and want to add a 300-hour certification, becoming a 500-hour Certified Yoga Teacher (CYT) registered with Yoga Alliance. Complete all required modules plus two electives.

The curriculum

What you'll study

Module breakdown

Inside each module

Each module combines pre-work (online lectures and reading), intensive in-person weekends, and monthly online wānanga for ongoing integration. Here's a summary of what each module covers.

Required modules

Mantra, Pranayama & Meditation

25 hrs Required

Sat 5 & Sat 26 September 2026
9am–5pm + online content

Explores the foundational theory, embodied practice, and teaching methodology of pranayama, meditation, and mantra. Covers breathing physiology and the subtle body, foundational meditation techniques across multiple lineages, the ethics of mantra, and safe trauma-aware instruction. Includes extensive practice teaching time with peer feedback.

Anatomy: Legs, Hips & Pelvis

35 hrs Required

Fri–Sun, 22–24 January 2027
8am–6pm daily

In-depth applied anatomy of the lower body — feet, knees, and hips — with a focus on how these structures function in yoga asana. Covers common injuries, contraindications, pain and injury management, and postural modifications. Includes a post-course written assessment.

Anatomy: Spine & Core

35 hrs Required

Fri–Sun, 11–13 June 2027
8am–6pm daily

A deep dive into the five-component core system (diaphragm, abdominals, pelvic floor, posterior core, and psoas) and spinal mechanics. Covers lower back conditions, sacroiliac joint management, inversions, and intelligent sequencing for injury prevention. Includes a post-course written assessment.

Anatomy: Shoulders & Neck

35 hrs Required

Fri–Sun, 23–25 July 2027
8am–6pm daily

Detailed applied anatomy of the shoulder complex, rotator cuff, scapular movement, cervical spine, and upper arm. Covers common impingements and injury management, safe weight-bearing alignment in asana, wrist support, and teaching methodology for chaturanga and other demanding poses. Includes a post-course written assessment.

Yoga Philosophy Part I

35 hrs Required

Sat 27 Feb · Sun 28 Feb · Mon 8 & 22 Mar 2027
Sat 8am–4pm · Sun 8am–5pm · Mon 5:30–8:30pm

An in-depth study of Patanjali's Yoga Sutras, the Bhagavad Gita, and Yogananda's Autobiography of a Yogi. Examines the 16 functions of the mind, classical Indian philosophical frameworks, different paths to liberation, and the relevance of classical philosophy for contemporary teachers. Includes 10 non-contact hours for required reading and a written reflective essay.

Yoga Philosophy, Lifestyle & Ethics Part II

25 hrs Required

Sat & Sun, 3–4 April 2027
8am–5pm

Explores Ayurveda basics, the diverse historical roots of yoga (drawing on Mallinson & Singleton's Roots of Yoga), yoga through the lens of Western science, business ethics through a yogic framework, and the responsibilities of acknowledging indigeneity in yoga teaching. Includes 6 non-contact hours for required reading.

The (He)art of Sequencing

25 hrs Required

Sat–Sun, 13–14 March 2027  ·  repeat: 21–22 August 2027
8am–6pm

Advanced vinyasa sequencing methodology covering two AWHI templates (Peak Pose and Vinyasa Flow), curriculum design for sustained teaching, theming with philosophical depth, and common sequencing imbalances. Includes the business of yoga: workshops, courses, marketing, and teacher sustainability. Assessment: sequence submission using one of the two templates.

Teaching Practicum

25 hrs Required

Fri–Sun, 10–12 September 2027
Fri 4–8pm · Sat & Sun 8am–6pm

Three intensive sessions refining teaching presence, voice, assists, feedback skills, and professional readiness. Includes an in-depth assists workshop, inclusive and trauma-aware teaching practice, and a final 45–60 minute assessed class. Assessment: teach a full public class.

Online Wānanga

20 hrs Required

Monthly throughout 2026–2027

Monthly Zoom calls aligned with each module — one per required module and one per elective. Each call opens with meditation, pranayama, or movement, followed by student-led Q&A and wānanga discussion. All calls are recorded for those unable to attend live.

Wrap-Up & Graduation

10 hrs Required

Saturday, 23 October 2027
8am–8pm

A full day of integration, reflection, and ceremony. Includes a final group asana practice, embodied reflection on the training journey, professional development discussion, final witnessed teaching practicum, and a formal graduation ceremony.

Electives

Yoga Nidra

15 hrs Elective

1 May 2027 (self-paced online)
Live calls: Wed 5, 12 & 19 May, 7pm  ·  Repeat intake: 1 Sep + Wed 8, 15 & 22 Sep, 7pm

An online self-paced course with three weekly live Zoom calls. Covers the history and neuroscience of Yoga Nidra, classical and modern structuring approaches, trauma-aware and inclusive delivery, and sequencing for different contexts (class endings, standalone, workshops, retreats). Assessment: write and record your own 30–45 minute Yoga Nidra script.

Yoga Meets Matauranga Māori

15 hrs Elective

Wed 5, 12, 19 & 26 May 2027
5:30–8:45pm

Four evening sessions exploring the synergies between Matauranga Māori (Māori knowledge systems) and yoga philosophy. Structured around four concepts — Aro-ha, Wahangū, Nohopuku, and Māramatanga — each unpacked alongside related yoga teachings.

Prenatal Yoga

15 hrs Elective

Fri–Sun, 14–16 August 2026  ·  repeat: 18–20 June 2027
Fri 5:30–8:30pm · Sat 10am–6pm · Sun 12–6pm

A foundational training for teaching pregnant students in public classes. Covers hormonal and structural changes by trimester, pelvic floor anatomy, birth-supportive movement, common pregnancy ailments, contraindications, and inclusive trauma-aware cueing. Note: this module provides base-level preparation; it does not constitute a full prenatal yoga certification.

Yoga in ACTion

15 hrs Elective

Saturdays, Oct 3, 10, 17, 24, 31 & Nov 7, 14, 21 2026
9–10:30am

Eight online sessions integrating yoga, mindfulness, and Acceptance & Commitment Therapy (ACT). Based on the Mindfulness Yoga ACT (MYACT) protocol, this module provides evidence-based tools for supporting students living with stress, chronic pain, or emotional challenges. Not yoga therapy — practical classroom tools for resilience and values-aligned teaching.

Hands on Assists

15 hrs Elective

Fri–Sun, 30 Oct–1 Nov 2026
Fri 4:30–8pm · Sat 9am–4pm · Sun 8:30am–3pm

A practical intensive covering consent protocols, the three types of assists (verbal, energetic, and mechanical touch), safe and trauma-aware assist delivery, and inclusive touch practices. Provides extensive hands-on practice time with peer feedback.

Completing the programme

Assessment & certification

Module assessments

Most modules include a post-course written or practical assessment (short answer exam, sequencing submission, or recorded class). Passing mark is 60% or higher.

Full certification

Complete all required modules, two electives, and all assessments to receive your 300hr Vinyasa YTT Certificate from AWHI Yoga School and qualify for Yoga Alliance RYT 500 registration.

Individual CE credits

Take individual modules without pursuing full certification. Each completed module (with assessment) delivers Yoga Alliance Continuing Education (CE) hours.
