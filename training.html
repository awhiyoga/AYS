Rooted in two worlds.

Our trainings explore the beautiful marriage between ancient yoga and Te Ao Māori teachings, following the Māori model of hauora through the four pillars of Te Whare Tapa Whā. Whether you're training to teach or deepening your personal practice — haere mai! This course is meant for you.

Flagship programme

200hr Vinyasa
Yoga Teacher Training

Dates 30 July – 15 November 2026
Investment $4,599 NZD
Accreditation Yoga Alliance RYT 200
Format Five weekend intensives, 8am–6pm

Our Yoga Alliance accredited 200hr YTT is a comprehensive journey into becoming a certified yoga teacher — or for anyone looking to go deeper into their practice. Structured around the five pillars of Te Whare Tapa Whā, it is a uniquely Aotearoa experience that honours both yoga tradition and Māori wisdom.

All bodies and experience levels welcome but a basic vinyasa knowledge recommended (approximately 6 months of practice).

Diversity & Inclusion

Fully funded scholarships

We believe yoga teacher training should be accessible to everyone. Our scholarship programme offers 100% tuition funding for those from communities underrepresented in yoga spaces.

Scholarships cover full tuition, all required textbooks, unlimited AWHI membership during training, and lifetime membership discounts.

Apply for scholarship

Who is eligible?

  • Māori and Pasifika peoples
  • BIPOC individuals
  • LGBTQ+ community members
  • Other underrepresented groups in yoga
  • NZ Citizens or Permanent Residents only

Your teachers

Meet the facilitators

Kathleen Kuehn

she/her

RYT 500 · Co-owner & Lead Facilitator

500-ERYT with certifications in vinyasa, yin, yoga nidra, level-one Pilates, and Hot HIIT Pilates. PhD in Media & Communications with nearly two decades of tertiary teaching experience. Known for her sharp wit and playful approach, Kathleen creates an environment where deep self-inquiry and genuine laughter coexist — because growth doesn't have to be serious.

Tom Brotherstone

he/him

E-RYT 500 · Co-owner & Lead Facilitator

500-ERYT with training completed in Rishikesh, India. Over a decade teaching yin and vinyasa. Tom brings an easeful, approachable facilitation style with warmth and humour that puts students immediately at ease. His classes weave yoga philosophy throughout, grounding movement in meaning.

Murray Hemi

he/him

RYT 200 · Trauma Specialist

Psychotherapist and trauma specialist with training from the Yoga Education in Prison Trust and Hakomi Institute. Strong mātauranga Māori background. Current practice focuses on men's relationship coaching and group facilitation.
