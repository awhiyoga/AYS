Strengthen your workplace with wellbeing. AWHI offers a range of programmes designed to support the health and hauora of your team — at our studio, at your workplace, or online.

What we offer

Three ways to bring
wellbeing to your team.

Our Workplace Wellness programme is built around Te Whare Tapa Whā — honouring the four dimensions of wellbeing: body, mind, spirit, and community. Whether you're looking for discounted class passes for your staff, a one-off private group session, or a workshop on stress and mindfulness, we'll work with you to find the right fit.

All offerings can be delivered at our Wellington CBD studio, at your workplace, or as a webinar. Get in touch to discuss a custom programme for your organisation.

Option 3

Educational Presentations
& Workshops

Our workshops and seminars are delivered by highly qualified senior facilitators — as webinars, face-to-face (at our studio or your offices), or in a dual-delivery format. Tailored to corporate and organisational environments.

Presentations

Shorter informational seminars — generally 30–60 minutes — that introduce key concepts without requiring pre/post assessments or movement practice. Conclude with a Q&A.

Workshops

Longer, practice-based offerings that can be adapted to run as 1–2 hour seminars, half-day, full-day, or multi-day interactive courses. Include movement and/or meditation, practical activities, and applicable tools for everyday life.

The Science of Stress, Yoga & Meditation

Introduces the science behind the benefits of yoga and meditation — including the effects of work-related stress and burnout on the nervous system, current research on yoga and meditation, and the relationship between mindfulness and hauora.

Delivered as a 30–60 min seminar or webinar. We discuss yoga and meditation through the kaupapa of Te Whare Tapa Whā.

Covers

  • Work-related burnout
  • Stress and the nervous system
  • Mindfulness
  • Empirical support for yoga & meditation
  • Te Whare Tapa Whā integration

Mindfulness & Meditation

Introduces the basics of mindfulness and meditation — the 'what, why and how' of beginning a practice. Participants are taken through mindfulness techniques including dyadic conversation, journaling, breathwork, and meditation.

Available as a 30–60 min presentation or a longer practice-based workshop (1–2 hours, half-day, or multi-day).

Covers

  • Stress and health
  • Mindfulness
  • Journaling as self-inquiry
  • Breathwork
  • Meditation techniques

Engagement at Work: Strengths & Values

Explores the connections between performance and engagement, offering tools for creating an upwards spiral of positive emotion and engagement in the workplace. Grounded in positive psychology research on flow and strengths.

Adaptable to a 1–2 hour seminar, half-day, or full-day format.

Covers

  • Engagement at work
  • Flow theory of optimal performance
  • Digital wellbeing
  • Using strengths at work
